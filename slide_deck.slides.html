Analyzing Client Bike Rentals¶

by Deepak Nandipati¶

Investigation Overview¶

My analysis of the datasets was to determine which group of individuals are likely to have the longest rental duration and to find out what timespread these riders are most active in San Franciso.

Dataset Overview¶

This dataset contains of trip information of more than 400,000 data points of bike rentals from the year 2017, data consists from June 2017 to December 2017. Using variables such as start_times and end_times and along with user details we can deduce many conclusions. Data was thoroughly cleaned from null values, duplicates and funneled to answer our necessary questions.

Determining the busiest times of the day¶

In analysis there was a particular pattern that was observed, which was of the time of day the rentals were most active. Rentals are most accurate during the business hours with most rentals starting during work times and ending after work. This shows us that most rentals are working class people using it for commuting. The graph below has a bimodal shape, which indicates to us that it has two peaks - one at 8:00AM (count of 54,086) and another at 5:00PM (count of 53,828).

The other graph below will indicate the count for days of the week. Monday to Friday being the busiest, which further supports our theory of bike rentals are being used for commuting to work.

Subscription type vs. Average trip duration¶

Customers and Subscribers had an interesting differences in average durations. It is shown that Subscribers have less rental time compared to the customer durations, this may be because subscribers only need the rentals for daily commuting while a typical customer may only use it for leisure drive.

Subscription type and Genders vs. Average trip duration¶

The plot shows brief summary of the distrubtion of data by comparing it to variables such as start time, duration of rental and riders age.

Start Day

  • Mondays are the most verstalise with age ranges, followed by saturday
  • Sundays appear to have the greatest duration of bike rentals, data is what we may expect for a weekend
  • Start hours of rentals are very versatile with the days of the week, but weekends are generally later started than weekdays. Strange how Thursdays have a narrow starting time.

Start Month

  • November has the most diverse age group, which makes sense as November is the second busiest of the months and more rentals are being used
  • Although November is not the busiest it does have the greatest duration of rentals, this can indicate to us that diversified age groups and duration have a certain correlation
  • No correlation or patterns to be found between months of rental and start of bike rental

Member_age_groups

  • age groups of 10-20 and 60-70 are very minimal that plots for 10-20 are not even visible
  • Interestingly, it appears that age groups 60-70 rent the bike for the longest time, compared to the other groups
